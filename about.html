I'm only an aspiring programmer/developer born and raised in the Philippines, in hopes to become something more, and to find out more about myself. More or less, I'm still trying to explore that hidden spark in me, and that I always have something to prove.

My personal interests are webriddles, online games, platformers, first-person shooters and gacha games. I started getting into computers since I was a child, at the age of 9 years old, playing built-in software games like Purble Place. I also got interested in playing intellectual-based games in the past like Bookworm Adventures 1 and 2. After that, I've discovered more games to play and fell in love with both Roblox and Minecraft. Since Minecraft was not free before and I could not afford the money to buy it, I was playing Roblox which made my entire childhood and what has shaped me into what I am today. I met friends, made memories, had lots of experience, and soon enough, this passion for gaming has turned to a passion to making or developing for one. I took this course because I thought that I could make games that would leave an impression for everyone that plays it.

In an attempt to develop a game myself, I learned Roblox Studio's basic components. I struggled with coding at first, so I had to lean more onto designing and modelling at the start. At the time I went to high school, I was becoming better at designing, but over time I realized how badly I needed to learn the fundamentals of programming to really make something out of nothing. So I've tried and got the hang of it but still believed that it wasn't enough. After graduating from high school, I chose to pursue this dream to college where I now sit, writing this line of code today.

I continued working through projects within ROBLOX, and was hired to become a developer in a game. I reluctantly agreed, as it would help me gain experience in a team setting and a work environment. It was a wonderful first-hand experience, I got to create my imagination, I was allowed to tinker some aspects to further fit my vision, and alas, it all worked out fine. Although the game has not reached a big success, I was still delighted to be a part of something even if it was short-lived.
